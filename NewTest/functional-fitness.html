Functional Fitness Self-Assessment

How Functional Are You?

Functional fitness is your ability to move safely and confidently in everyday life — getting up from a chair, carrying groceries, climbing stairs, catching yourself from a fall. This self-assessment evaluates your strength, balance, mobility, and endurance without any equipment. Be honest for the most accurate result.

💪

Lower Body Strength — Chair Stand Test

How to do it: Sit in a chair with arms crossed on your chest. Stand fully upright and sit back down. Count how many times you can do this in 30 seconds.
🦶

Balance — Single-Leg Stand

How to do it: Stand near a wall for safety. Lift one foot a few inches off the ground and balance on the other leg. Time how long you can hold it with eyes open.
🚶

Mobility & Agility — Timed Up & Go

How to do it: Sit in a chair. On "go," stand up, walk 10 feet, turn around, walk back, and sit down. Time yourself in seconds.
🏋️

Upper Body Strength — Arm Curl Test

How to do it: Hold a weight (5 lb for women, 8 lb for men — or estimate with a bag). Do as many bicep curls as you can in 30 seconds with full range of motion.
🧘

Flexibility — Sit & Reach

How to do it: Sit on the edge of a chair, extend one leg straight, and reach both hands toward your toes. How far can you reach?
❤️

Endurance — 2-Minute Step Test

How to do it: March in place for 2 minutes, raising your knees to mid-thigh height. Count how many times your right knee reaches that height.
